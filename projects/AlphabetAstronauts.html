Educational Space Adventure Typing Game


Introduction In today's digital world, teaching school children how to type has become increasingly important due to the various social benefits it provides. Typing offers many social benefits for students, one of which is improved communication. It encourages them to communicate more frequently, which can help develop their social and emotional intelligence. In addition to emotional intelligence, students will gain career readiness. By teaching children to type at an early age, they can gain a head start in their career development and be better prepared for future employment opportunities. Typing can be a more accessible way to communicate for students with learning disabilities or physical impairments. It can also make it easier for children who struggle with writing to express themselves. Overall, teaching elementary school children how to type is a valuable investment that offers social benefits and equips them with a crucial skill for their future success.
Game Components We offer a fun and interactive game as a way to teach children these important typing skills. Our game will feature the following major components:
  1. Main Menu:
    • Store a text file containing what level player is on and how many points they have when they close the game
    • Has options to start from the beginning or start from the level where they left off
  2. Level Overworld:
    • Navigable area between levels
    • Player can jump around to platforms and collect tokens worth points
    • Allows player to get to the next level
  3. Four Replayable Levels:
    • Level tracker
    • Point tracker
    • Levels each follow the storyline
  4. Ability to take input from the keyboard, track typing speed, and accuracy:
    • Randomized word or phrase appears on screen (from word bank) of which the player must type correctly under the time limit
    • Player can backspace when typing and must press enter to submit
    • Level advances only when word/phrase is typed correctly
