A Biomimetic jellyfish inspired soft robot


Introduction Typically researchers use robotic underwater vehicles to track and study aquatic life. These robots are robust to incident currents underwater, but the wake from their propellers may disrupt the ecosystem the robot intends to study. To make aquatic environmental monitoring systems less obtrusive, we propose using a jellyfish-inspired robot to study marine life. We hypothesize that the wake from jellyfish-inspired robots will be less turbulent and therefore reduce the disturbance to the ecosystem. To do this, we will use an electromagnet-driven propulsion system that will theoretically be much quieter than motor-driven systems.
